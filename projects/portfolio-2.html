Overview

Reliable transfer protocol

DRTP is a reliable file transfer protocol built on top of UDP. The report explains the trade-off between speed and reliability and sets the goal of transferring text and images while ensuring delivery.

The implementation adds a three-way handshake and reliability modes such as Stop-and-Wait, Go-Back-N, and Selective Repeat with configurable window size. Tests capture transfer time, data volume, and bandwidth, with command-line flags to configure host, file, and mode.
